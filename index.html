Introduction

Rocket League is an online game developed by Psyonix and released on 7th July 2015. The game was released as a follow-up to "Supersonic Acrobatic Rocket-Powered Battle-Cars". Since its release, Rocket League has become a very popular online e-sports game â€” most notably after its decision to switch to a free-to-play model. It is available on PC, Playstation, Xbox, and Nintendo Switch.

Competitive aspects of the game require extensive knowledge in order to perform.

Gameplay Modes

Rocket League consists of three game modes:

  • 3v3
  • 2v2
  • 1v1

These game modes can be played in both a ranked and casual environment. There are separate modes available as casual-only: Rumble, hoops, snowday, and dropshot.

Ranked Matchmaking

Rocket league utilises a ranked matchmaking system that recalibrates MMR (Match-making rating) after each win and loss. This MMR determines the skill of opponents that each player will face.

In each game mode, ranks will be determined by a player's MMR.

  • Bronze
  • Silver
  • Gold
  • Platinum
  • Diamond
  • Champ
  • Grand Champ
  • Supersonic Legend (SSL)
Boost

Boost allows players to rapidly increase forward momentum and perform aerial movements. Using boost will deplete the gauge located at the bottom-right of the user's screen. Boost has a minimum of 0% in which case the user can no longer use boost. There is also a maximum boost of 100%.

Boost can be added to a player's boost gauge by driving over the available boost pads. Smaller boost pads will recharge 12%, while larger boost pads give 100%. Only six boost pads are available on the field.

Boost can be consumed while jumping to perform aerials, changing the dynamic of gameplay.
Aerials

Aerials are classified as a beginner mechanic that players are able to perform in order to reach the ball when it is too high too reach.

To perform an aerial:
Jump + Boost + Joystick Down

A fast aerial can be performed by using jump once airborne and performing an aerial. Note: Players must release the joystick when jumping on the second instance. Fast Aerials allow players to reach heights even faster.
Flip Resets

Flip resets are a high level mechanic which allow players to utilise a double-jump. While a double-jump can be gained by driving on a flat surface, players must aerial onto a mid-air ball and aim their car to land flat on the ball using air-roll and the joystick.

Flip-resets can be performed in conjuction with air-dribbles in order to score the ball.
